Flood Safety Tips

  • Know your risk: Check if your home is located in a flood-prone area.
  • Prepare an emergency kit: Include a first aid kit, non-perishable food, and water for at least three days.
  • Have an evacuation plan: Know the routes to higher ground and have a plan for your pets as well.
  • Turn off electricity and gas: This will prevent electrocution and fire hazards.
  • Keep important documents safe: Store important documents in a waterproof container or upload them to a cloud service.
  • Stay informed: Monitor news and weather reports for updates and warnings.
  • Don't drive through floodwaters: It only takes six inches of water to cause a car to lose control and potentially stall.
  • Seek higher ground: If you're caught in a flash flood, seek higher ground immediately and avoid walking through moving water.

What to Do After a Flood

After a flood, it's important to stay safe and start the recovery process as soon as possible. Here are some tips:

  • Stay away from floodwaters: Even after the water recedes, it may be contaminated with hazardous materials.
  • Check for damage: Inspect your home for damage and document it with photographs or video.
  • Contact your insurance company: Report any damage to your insurance company and keep records of your communication.
  • Start the cleanup process: Begin by removing any standing water and then clean and disinfect all surfaces that came into contact with floodwaters.
